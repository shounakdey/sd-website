About Me

Hello! I am Shounak Dey, a PhD Candidate in the Engineering Mechanics Unit at JNCASR, Bengaluru. My research focuses on Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), Heat Transfer, and Numerical Methods, exploring complex flow phenomena using computational and experimental approaches.

Currently, I am working on the numerical investigation of two-layer Rayleigh-Bénard-Marangoni (RBM) flows using the Chebyshev spectral method to study convective instabilities in multi-layer fluid systems. Additionally, I have conducted an experimental investigation of water droplet contact angles on different substrates, contributing to surface wettability research.

I also gained research experience as an intern at the University of Bordeaux, where I helped develop a spectral solver for the Cahn-Hilliard equation, used in phase separation and interfacial dynamics studies.

This website showcases my academic journey, research projects, and contributions. I look forward to connecting with researchers and professionals in related fields. Feel free to explore my work and reach out for discussions or collaborations!
